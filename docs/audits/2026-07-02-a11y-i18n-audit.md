## Executive summary

- **6 CRITICAL, 9 MAJOR, 11 MINOR** findings.
- **~49 hardcoded Dutch-only UI strings** in learner-facing chrome outside `i18n.ts` (excludes the
  29 bespoke lesson pages, counted separately below, and excludes ~10 admin-only strings).
- Two structurally distinct i18n gaps, easy to conflate — kept separate throughout:
  1. **Chrome that ignores the `userLanguage`/profile setting** even though `i18n.ts` already has the
     key (a wiring bug — cheap to fix).
  2. **Content that has no English column in the schema at all** (grammar-exercise payloads,
     dialogue-cloze translations) — a data-model gap, not a wiring gap.
- The single biggest accessibility gap: **a correct answer produces zero non-visual feedback.**
  `useExerciseScoring.ts` only routes wrong/fuzzy outcomes through the one component with `aria-live`
  regions (`ExerciseFeedback`); correct answers auto-advance in silence
  (`src/components/experience/ExperiencePlayer.tsx:213-215` vs `:216-234`). A screen-reader user who
  answers correctly hears nothing until the next question's heading auto-announces.
- The single biggest i18n gap: **`RecapScreen.tsx` — the screen every session ends on — is 100%
  hardcoded Dutch and doesn't even accept a `userLanguage` prop** (`src/components/experience/
  RecapScreen.tsx:6-14`). Every session an English-preference learner completes ends in un-toggleable
  Dutch.
- What already works well and should **not** be touched: the wrong/fuzzy feedback path
  (`ExerciseFeedback.tsx`) is a genuinely well-built a11y surface — `role="status" aria-live="assertive"`
  full-sentence announcements, icon+color+text (never color-alone) on MCQ options, comprehensive
  `prefers-reduced-motion` guards on every animation, and an explicit WCAG-AA contrast audit for the
  light-mode correct/wrong tokens (`src/main.tsx:266-268`). `i18n.ts` itself is comprehensive and has
  full NL/EN parity for ~400 keys — the problem is components that don't call into it, not the bundle
  itself.

### The 29 bespoke lesson pages — a structurally separate, much larger gap

Every lesson under `/lesson/:id` renders a hand-authored bespoke page (`src/pages/lessons/lesson-N/Page.tsx`, generated by the `lesson-page-designer` agent from that lesson's `content.json`). **Zero of the 29 pages import `useT`, read `userLanguage`, or reference `translations[...]`** (verified: `grep -rL "useT\|userLanguage\|translations\[" src/pages/lessons/*/Page.tsx` returns all 29 files). Every section title, dialogue eyebrow, aria-label, and play-button label is inline Dutch prose (e.g. `lesson-7/Page.tsx:59` `aria-label={playing ? 'Stop' : 'Speel uit'}`, `:92-93` section titles).

The underlying `content.json` **does** carry English fields — `meta.lesson_audio_url_en`, `sections[].content.lines[].translation_en`, `sections[].content.items[].english` (confirmed by inspecting `lesson-7/content.json`) — but across all 29 `Page.tsx` files, the **only** field actually consumed is `lesson_audio_url_en` (wired into `LessonGrammarAudioBand`, `lesson-7/Page.tsx:635`, `lesson-1/Page.tsx:325`). `translation_en`/`english` per-line/per-item fields are read in exactly **one** place, `lesson-29/Page.tsx:122`, and even there it's rendered unconditionally alongside the Dutch text (a bilingual display), not gated on `userLanguage`. This means the data pipeline already produces EN content for lesson prose that the UI simply never surfaces — a wiring gap, not a content gap, for this specific slice.

**CRIT-3 — CRITICAL**: an English-preference learner opening any of the 29 lesson reader pages (the primary "Lessen" content surface) sees 100% Dutch prose, section titles, and button labels, with no way to switch — despite the data existing to do so for dialogue lines and vocab glosses.

### Exercise content language assumptions — schema-level gap, not just wiring

Grepping `scripts/migration.sql` for every `*_en` column shows the data model is **not** uniformly bilingual. Two tiers exist:

**Tier 1 — has full NL/EN parity (schema + pipeline):**
- `learning_items.translation_nl` / `translation_en` (`migration.sql:2772-2779`, collapses the old `item_meanings` row-per-language table, which itself has `translation_language CHECK IN ('en','nl')` at `:245` with both rows populated)
- `lesson_dialogue_lines.translation_nl` / `translation_en` (`migration.sql:3442-3465`)
- `lesson_section_grammar_categories.title_en` / `rules_en` (`migration.sql:3182-3217`)
- `item_morphology.gloss_nl` / `gloss_en` (`migration.sql:194-195`)
- `affixed_form_pairs.derived_gloss_nl` / `derived_gloss_en` (`migration.sql:3338-3340`)
- `lessons.audio_path_en` (grammar podcast, `migration.sql:104-110`), `texts.audio_path_en` (pronunciation podcast, `migration.sql:162-167`)

These are the tables the exercise implementations actually read from (`RecognitionMCQ.tsx:36-37`, `CuedRecallExercise.tsx` etc. all filter `meanings.find(m => m.translation_language === userLanguage)`), and they're correctly wired.

**Tier 2 — single-language only, no `_en` column exists at all:**
- `contrast_pair_exercises.{prompt_text,target_meaning,explanation_text}` (`migration.sql:2948-2960`)
- `sentence_transformation_exercises.{transformation_instruction,hint_text,explanation_text}` (`migration.sql:2983-2995`)
- `constrained_translation_exercises.{source_language_sentence,explanation_text}` (`migration.sql:3017-3029`)
- `cloze_mcq_exercises.{translation,explanation_text}` (`migration.sql:3052-3064`)
- `dialogue_clozes.translation_text` (`migration.sql:2852-2861`)

**CRIT-4 — CRITICAL**: `.claude/agents/grammar-exercise-creator.md:100-192` is explicit and unambiguous — every one of these fields is instructed to be authored **in Dutch** ("Dutch — communicative situation", "Dutch — short gloss", "explain WHY one is correct... Dutch", "Dutch sentence to translate"). There is no English variant anywhere in the authoring→schema→render chain for grammar exercises (`ClozeMcq`, `ContrastPairExercise`, `SentenceTransformationExercise`, `ConstrainedTranslationExercise`) or the dialogue-based vocab cloze (`Cloze.tsx`). An English-preference learner doing **any** grammar exercise — which is a large share of total exercise volume from lesson 6 onward per the capability model — sees the Indonesian prompt (language-neutral) but the prompt framing, the "why" explanation, and the pre-answer translation are Dutch, unconditionally, regardless of profile language.

### Summary for the "EN-first customer" question

The data model is **not** symmetric NL/EN — it's NL-primary with EN retrofitted per-table as each table was migrated (ADR 0011/0012 language). Coverage, factually:

| Content type | EN at the schema level? |
|---|---|
| Vocabulary item meanings (recognition/recall/listening MCQ, dictation) | Yes — full parity |
| Dialogue-line translations (lesson reader + cloze prompts) | Yes at the table (`lesson_dialogue_lines.translation_en`); **not** on the derived `dialogue_clozes.translation_text` used at exercise time |
| Morphology/affix glosses | Yes — full parity |
| Lesson grammar-section rule text | Yes — `title_en`/`rules_en` |
| Grammar podcast / pronunciation podcast audio | Yes — `audio_path_en` twin tracks |
| **Grammar exercises** (contrast pair, sentence transformation, constrained translation, cloze MCQ) | **No** — Dutch only, by explicit agent instruction, no schema column exists |
| **Bespoke lesson reader pages** (prose, section titles, chrome) | Data exists for dialogue/vocab lines; **not consumed** by any of the 29 pages except one partial case |
| **App chrome around sessions** (progress header, recap screen, lesson CTA labels, several error/loading states) | `i18n.ts` has EN strings; **many call sites don't use them** |

So the honest answer to "how far does EN content coverage extend" is: **vocabulary drilling is EN-complete; grammar drilling and the lesson-reading experience are not**, and on top of that, several pieces of session *chrome* (not lesson *content*) that already have EN copy sitting in `i18n.ts` are simply not wired to it.

## Top 5 fixes by impact / effort

1. **Wire `ExperiencePlayer`'s progress header + `Session.tsx`'s error/loading strings to the existing `i18n.ts` keys.** (`ExperiencePlayer.tsx:59`, `Session.tsx:114,121,231,241,256,274,299-300`) — highest-visibility surface (every question, every session), and the keys already exist for most of it. Cheapest, highest-impact fix in this audit.
2. **Give `RecapScreen` a `userLanguage` prop and route its ~14 strings through `i18n.ts`.** Every session ends here; currently un-toggleable regardless of profile setting.
3. **Route correct-answer commits through a (new, brief) `aria-live="polite"` announcement**, e.g. a lightweight status region fired alongside the existing 1500ms `MARK_PROCESSING`→`commit('correct')` timer in `useExerciseScoring.ts`, so screen-reader users get *some* non-visual confirmation instead of silence. Doesn't need the full `ExerciseFeedback` screen — a one-line polite announcement closes most of the gap.

## Verdicts

**English-speaking learner, keyboard and screen-reader unrestricted:** Can complete a full session for its *drill mechanics* (options, typing, submitting all work; the underlying vocabulary content is EN-complete), but will see Dutch leak through constantly: the "Oefening X van Y" progress header on every card, the recap screen at the end of every single session, any grammar exercise's framing/explanation text (contrast pair, sentence transformation, translation, cloze MCQ — Dutch by design, no EN column exists), and — if they ever open a lesson to read rather than just drill — all 29 lesson reader pages in full. This is not a broken app for this user, but it repeatedly and visibly breaks the promise of the language toggle; a preview customer evaluating the app in English would notice within their first session.

**Keyboard-only learner (no screen reader):** Can complete a full session with no functional blocker. Every interactive element is a native, focusable control; Enter/Space/Tab all behave correctly; the correct-answer pause doesn't strand focus; the feedback screen's Continue button receives focus automatically. The only friction is the absence of a skip-link (a few extra Tab presses per page) — an inconvenience, not a barrier.

**Screen-reader user:** Can technically finish a session — nothing throws or dead-ends — but the experience actively undersells the app's own correctness signal: every correct answer is silent (CRIT-1), and every exercise's ambient landmark/group/button names are read in Dutch regardless of language preference (MAJ-2) on top of the nested-`<main>` landmark confusion (MAJ-1). A screen-reader user relying purely on audio cues would frequently be uncertain whether they got an answer right until the next question started, and — if they also can't hear the target-language audio — currently has no working accommodation at all for listening/dictation cards (CRIT-2), despite the app claiming to offer one.
