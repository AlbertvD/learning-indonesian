**Implications.** (a) The "serious Indonesian app / depth" positioning survives contact with reality: *every* mass-market option caps at A2, and reviewers write "what to do after Duolingo Indonesian" articles — the post-A2 vacuum is real and named in the market. (b) Bet 3 (Percakapan) has a named competitor (Kaiwa): the differentiation MUST be the learner-model constraint + FSRS integration, and its spec now says so. (c) A whole niche app (KataKita) exists just for colloquial Indonesian — evidence the register gap is monetizable (see §3, the biggest new insight).

## 2. Market sizing (verified where possible)

- **Indo community NL:** ~1.5–2M people with Indische/Molukse roots (≈1.7M in 2021; 8–10% of the NL population). The heritage thesis holds at full size.
- **Dutch tourists to Bali:** ⚠️ TEMPER — ~50–60k/yr in the last solid data (2009–2012); NL is *not* in Bali's current top-10 source markets. Total NL→Indonesia travel (Java, family/roots visits) is larger, but "vacation volume top-of-funnel" is a **modest** channel, not a huge one. Heritage remains the primary NL segment; vacation is secondary.
- **Bali long-stay foreigners (EN wedge):** ~35k+ digital nomads; tens of thousands of expats concentrated in Canggu/Ubud/Sanur; a **5-year digital-nomad visa launched mid-2025** — the long-stay population is growing and now commits to *years*, which matches a subscription horizon. EN wedge segment 1 validated and strengthening.
- **Australia:** Indonesian-language schooling has **collapsed** (>80% enrolment decline since early 2000s; <200 Year-12 students nationwide in 2019; programs still closing 2024–25). REFRAME Bet 5's Australia angle: not a schools sales channel — a **supply vacuum** (motivated adults have nowhere to learn) plus a live national "Indonesia capability" policy anxiety that makes for grant/institutional angles and press narrative, not B2C volume.
- **NL willingness-to-pay anchors:** Volksuniversiteit lists Indonesisch among its *most popular* language courses (standing demand signal); classroom courses run to hundreds of euros; privéles €17/hr (Superprof) to €87.50/hr (Bogaers); NHA sells a B1 thuisstudie. An app at €8–12/mo is *cheap* against every local alternative. Benchmark app pricing: $10–20/mo (Ling/Pimsleur), $4–23 tiers (Pod101).

## 3. THE new product insight: the bahasa gaul gap ⭐

The single most consistent criticism of existing tools: they teach **formal bahasa baku only**, while spoken Indonesian runs on **bahasa gaul** — different pronouns (*gue/lu*, *aku*), negation (*nggak*), aspect words (*udah, lagi*), and the particles that dominate real speech (*dong, sih, kok, deh, banget*). A Duolingo graduate "sounds like a news broadcast and can't understand the reply."

**Our exposure:** the Leiden coursebook source is also standard/formal register — we likely share this gap with Duolingo today.

**The opportunity — "Spreektaal" track:** register pairs as capabilities. The learner already knows *tidak*; teach *nggak* as a linked colloquial variant (cheap wins riding existing knowledge — the loanword-bridge trick applied to register); particle micro-lessons; and **register-aware Percakapan personas** (the friend speaks gaul, the immigration officer speaks baku — teaching register *choice*, which no competitor does). This composes with the capability model exactly like loanwords do, it is exercise-able and schedulable, and it answers the market's loudest documented complaint. **Promotion candidate to the bets list.**

## 4. Sharpened commercial ideas (research-grounded)

1. **"Na Duolingo" as an explicit acquisition frame.** People search for "after Duolingo Indonesian" — and our placement test (Bet 1 slice 2) is *literally the product for that moment*: "Stuck at the end of Duolingo? Take the instaptoets, skip what you know, continue to B1." Add EN SEO pages targeting exactly these queries (growth-layer leg 1 gains its highest-intent page type — competitor-comparison/exit-ramp content).
2. **Publish the "route to B1" map.** Every competitor caps at A2 — make our post-A2 depth *visible and public* (curriculum map + the CEFR estimate from the Voortgang review, I5). "The only app that takes you past A2" is checkable, honest, and unique.
3. **Pricing direction:** €9.99/mo or ~€79/yr sits mid-pack globally and reads as a bargain locally ("één privéles = drie maanden app"). Gift subscriptions (B5) inherit the same anchor.
4. **Bali B2B2C micro-channel:** coworking spaces (Canggu/Ubud/Sanur concentrations) + visa agencies as partner/perk distribution for the EN wedge — cheap to test, matches where the 5-year-visa audience physically is.
5. **Volksuniversiteit partnership (NL):** their Indonesian courses are demonstrably popular; the app as course companion / cohort license = B2B2C distribution through the exact institution serious NL learners already trust.
6. **Australia as narrative, not channel:** "the country lost its Indonesian-teaching infrastructure; here's the app filling the vacuum" — press/grant framing; deprioritize school B2C.
