# Graded-Reading / Comprehensible-Input Reader — Evidence Base & Design Principles

**Date:** 2026-06-28
**For:** Roadmap §B.5 (*Graded reading / comprehensible-input texts*) — the reading twin of podcasts. Reserved seam `lib/reading/` + `components/reading/` (foundation plan 2026-06-13, §80–86).
**Method:** Local-first. This doc starts from the SLA spine the project has **already** established (OpenBrain notes, ADRs 0007/0011/0014/0022, the 2026-04-25 sequencing research) and layers a focused web deep-research pass on the **reader-specific** questions those sources do not cover (glossing, encounter-frequency, incidental decay, reading-while-listening, the reading→SRS handoff). Every web claim was source-verified by a dedicated researcher; local claims cite the in-repo artifact.

> **Framing for the design phase:** the reader does **not** get to re-litigate coverage thresholds, Four Strands, or receptive-before-productive — those are settled locally and the web evidence confirms them. The reader's genuinely-new design surface is **glossing, recycling/encounter design, and the word→FSRS handoff.** Spend the design budget there.

**The architectural thesis this licenses (already implicit in the ADRs, now empirically grounded):** the reader is the **meaning-focused-input strand** — volume, recycling, rich context; the **FSRS capability spine is the deliberate strand** on the *same words*. Reader and SRS are complementary, not competing. Don't make reading do mastery (4× slower, perishable); make reading *feed* the SRS.

## Part 2 — The reader-specific evidence layer (new; from 2026-06-28 deep research)

### 2.1 Glossing — the core new design question

- **Glossing roughly *doubles* incidental vocabulary uptake** vs unglossed reading and does not hurt comprehension (modestly helps). Yanagisawa, Webb & Uchihara 2020 (359 effect sizes): 45%/33% words learned (immediate/delayed) glossed vs 27%/20% unglossed. He et al. 2026 pooled d≈0.94. **Strength: HIGH.** → Treat tap-to-gloss as a primary learning mechanism, not a convenience.
- **L1 (the learner's Dutch/English) glosses beat L2 (Indonesian) glosses for beginners**, strongest on immediate vocab tests, shrinking with proficiency (Kim, Lee & Lee 2024, g=.33). The app already stores NL **and** EN meanings → surface **NL-first by default**, with an L2/ID-definition mode for advanced learners. (Contested: Yanagisawa 2020 found no proficiency interaction — but L1-default for beginners is low-risk either way.)
- **Format matters and is counter-intuitive: always-shown inline glosses are the *weakest* format; multiple-choice "choose-then-verify" glosses are the *strongest*** (Yanagisawa 2020). Mechanism = Involvement Load (Laufer & Hulstijn 2001: need + search + evaluation) and the generation effect. A frictionless gloss removes the effort that builds the memory (Bjork & Kroll 2015 — the desirable-difficulty principle the project already cites).
- **Pop-up / tap-on-demand glosses get used and work** (Abraham 2008; Yun 2011). Keeping meaning *behind a deliberate tap* is itself the minimal "need" trigger — the learner registers the word as unknown before the answer appears.
- **Test-expectancy closes the loop**: telling learners that tapped words will be reviewed makes them engage glosses harder and improves form recall, at **no comprehension cost** (eye-tracking, PMC9851552). Re-encountering a glossed word 3–8× compounds it (He et al. 2026).

### 2.2 Encounter-frequency — how much recycling before a word "takes"

- The "needed encounters" figure is **genuinely contested** (6 / 8 / 10 / 12 / 20+ all appear). The decisive synthesis (Uchihara, Webb & Yanagisawa 2019, 45 ES): repetition has a **continuous, ~linear effect (r=.34) — "more is better," no clean threshold.** Single integers are study-specific artifacts.
- Useful anchors: Webb 2007 (1/3/7/10 encounters → broad gains by ~10); Waring & Takaki 2003 (~8 meetings for a 50% chance of *form* recognition at 3 months); Brown et al. 2008 ("sufficient" is "considerably higher than 7–9"). **Spacing** of the encounters is itself a significant moderator (Uchihara 2019).
- **Design read:** target a recycling **band of ~8–12 spaced encounters** of a word across reading material as a *heuristic*, not a guarantee. Do **not** hard-code one number, and do **not** assume learning from encounters — let the SRS verify mastery.

### 2.3 Incidental pickup is small and *perishable* — the empirical case for the SRS handoff

- Per-text pickup is modest and decays hard: Waring & Takaki 2003 — of 25 target words, ~1 word's meaning survived to 3 months; words met <8× retained ~nothing. Brown et al. 2008 — ~1 of 28 words on recall at 3 months. Form knowledge is acquired fast but decays fast; the rarer *meaning-recall* is slower to gain but more durable.
- Reading reaches **recognition** far faster than **recall/production**. → The reader is the *encounter generator/sensitiser*; **meaning-recall is precisely the dimension the SRS should own.** Hand words to SRS *soon* after the encounter band, before the decay curve drops them.

### 2.4 Reading-while-listening (the read-along the app already has)

- **RWL ≈ reading-only for vocabulary, both ≫ listening-only** (Brown, Waring & Donkaewbua 2008). Audio won't hurt uptake; it may help over volume (Webb & Chang 2015).
- **Comprehension is conditional, and this is contested:** RWL beats *listening-only* (Chang 2009, +10%) but can **underperform *silent reading*** at intermediate+ because the audio acts as a pacemaker that suppresses the re-reading/regressions silent reading allows (Hui & Godfroid 2026, preregistered + eye-tracked). The benefit is largest exactly where the learner *can't yet decode fluently*.
- **Indonesian-specific discount:** Indonesian has a **shallow, near-phonemic Latin orthography**, so for a Dutch/English-L1 learner the *decoding-rescue* value of audio is **smaller** than for English/Chinese (Koh et al. 2024, by extrapolation). The audio earns its place on **prosody, listening exposure, fluency-pacing, and engagement** — not decoding.
- **Affect is the cleanest win:** 72% preferred RWL (Brown 2008); the #1 quit reason is losing the freedom to pause and look words up.
- **Design read:** read-along **opt-in, per-text toggle**; **default OFF for comprehension-focused reading at A2+**, defensible ON for true beginners; **frictionless pause + tap-to-define that auto-pauses audio**; speed control; **phrase/sentence-level highlight by default**, word-level as a subtle optional mode (word-by-word karaoke risks over-binding the eyes to the audio pace). This refines — does not contradict — ADR 0022's word-level follow-along goal: word-level is the *follow-along* affordance, phrase-level is the safer default for *comprehension* reading.

### 2.5 The reading → SRS handoff (sentence mining)

- **Combining incidental reading + deliberate spaced study on the same words beats either alone** (Nation Four Strands — already a project principle).
- **Which words to push:** rank by **high-frequency × multiply-encountered × learner-looked-up**; gate on a frequency cutoff so rare words don't bloat the deck. The app *already* has frequency bands (top-100…top-1000) + FSRS capability state → it can compute "high-frequency AND not-yet-mastered AND tapped-while-reading" precisely.
- **Capture the source sentence as the carrier.** Sentence-mining practice + depth-of-processing theory favour **context/cloze cards over bare word↔gloss pairs**; the reader hands FSRS a free, authentic, i+1 carrier sentence. Reuses the existing dialogue-cloze model. (Strength: practitioner-consensus + theory-coherent; the *strict* "sentence cards beat word cards" RCT claim is mixed — hold as a strong default, not a law.)
- **Auto-*suggest*, not auto-add** — the lookup is a salience signal, but the learner should confirm what enters their deck.

## Part 3 — Design principles for the reader (the actionable output)

Each principle tags its evidence: **[local]** already-settled in-repo, **[web]** new this pass, **[both]** confirmed.

1. **Level texts per-learner by lexical coverage, not by a static CEFR tag.** Profile each text against *this* learner's FSRS-mastered Indonesian word-set; target the **95–98% known band**; auto-gloss the residual 2–5%; down-level/exclude anything <95%. Treat coverage as a gradient (95% assisted floor, 98% unassisted ceiling). **[both]**
2. **Two sub-cases, build (a) first.** (a) Re-present *existing* lesson text (dialogues + narrative sections) as a glossed reader — vocab already lives as capabilities, **no new schema**. (b) *New* content (dongeng/folktales already sourced) runs the harvest-to-vocab-theme loop and needs storage. (a) is the cheap, high-evidence MVP. **[local: foundation plan; roadmap §5]**
3. **Gloss = tap-on-demand, NL-first, never always-inline.** Default to the learner's NL gloss revealed by a deliberate tap; offer an ID-definition mode for advanced users. Inline-always-shown is the worst format — avoid it. **[web]**
4. **Make the gloss effortful: "choose-then-verify" beats "handed-the-answer."** Consider a two-tap reveal — first tap surfaces 2–3 plausible meaning options (forcing a guess/evaluation), second confirms. This reuses the app's existing **recognition_mcq distractor infrastructure** as gloss options. Keep a single-given gloss as a fast fallback. Highest-leverage design choice. **[web]**
5. **Engineer graduated friction, not maximum access.** Enough gloss to prevent wrong guesses and comprehension breakdown; enough effort to engage need/search/evaluation. Don't stack >2 gloss modalities (diminishing returns). **[web + local desirable-difficulty]**
6. **Recycle target vocabulary across consecutive texts (narrow reading).** Sequence by topic-cluster/series so high-value words recur within the ~8–12-encounter band the retention data demands. Cheap, high-leverage ordering rule that compounds with the SRS. **[web]**
7. **The reader feeds FSRS; it does not do mastery.** When a learner taps a word: log the encounter, and **auto-suggest** (not auto-add) an FSRS card for words that are **high-frequency × multiply-met × looked-up**, gated by a frequency cutoff. **Capture the source sentence and present the card as a context/cloze card**, reusing the dialogue-cloze model. **[both]**
8. **Frame tapped→reviewed explicitly.** "Words you look up get added to your reviews" measurably increases engagement and retention at no comprehension cost. **[web]**
9. **Read-along audio: opt-in, default OFF at A2+, phrase-level highlight, frictionless pause + auto-pausing tap-to-define, speed control.** Justify it on prosody/exposure/engagement, not decoding (shallow Indonesian orthography). Reuse the Story-podcast alignment substrate. **[web + local ADR 0022]**
10. **Offer re-read / read-along as fluency training** (track WPM across reads) for shorter/lower-level texts — text-level automaticity the word-level SRS doesn't touch. Don't over-claim vocabulary gains from it. **[web]**

## Part 4 — Contested points the design phase must decide (not settle here)

1. **95 vs 98 target band** — present as a gradient with a per-learner gloss-density response, not a single gate. The 98%-unassisted figure rests on a small (n=66) study now under replication. **[contested in the literature]**
2. **Single number for "encounters before SRS handoff"** — there isn't one. Use the ~8–12 band as a heuristic and let the SRS verify; make the trigger tunable. **[contested]**
3. **Sentence-context cards vs bare word cards** — strong default (cheaper for us, theory-backed), but not a clean RCT win; the carrier sentence must itself be ~i+1 or it distracts.
4. **Read-along default & highlight granularity** — reconcile ADR 0022's word-level follow-along *goal* with the web finding that word-level can over-bind the eyes during *comprehension* reading. Likely: word-level for the listening/follow-along mode, phrase-level (or off) for comprehension reading.
5. **Indonesian word-family definition** — coverage profiling must use an Indonesian frequency list (PBWL) with families defined by the project's root+affix model, not ported English BNC counts. Ties into the morphology module.

## Part 4b — Scope decisions locked with the user (2026-06-28)

These were settled in conversation after the research, and constrain the design phase:

1. **The reader is a separate "Read" mode**, not just tap-to-gloss bolted onto the podcast player. Rationale: it must support reading-only content the podcast surface cannot.
2. **Corpus = superset of the podcast corpus.** It re-presents the existing **story-podcast transcripts** (`podcasts.transcript_segments`, already ID/NL/EN-aligned + word-timed, CC BY) **and** adds **reading-only longer stories** that are too long/unsuitable to narrate as podcasts.
3. **Model the entity as a *Text with an optional audio face.*** A podcast = "a text that happens to have narration." Faces: 🎧 Listen (needs audio+timings) · 📖 Read (needs text+alignment; available to *any* text) · 🎴 Study-its-words (harvest→FSRS). This is the collections "one object, N faces" pattern, generalized.
4. **The unique value over the existing podcast read-along** (the design's reason to exist): (a) **per-word tap-to-gloss** on demand (the podcast only offers whole-sentence tab-switch = the weakest gloss format); (b) **vocab→FSRS harvest** (the podcast is listening-only, ADR 0022 §17; this is roadmap #4's deferred "PLUS" loop); (c) **silent self-paced reading** (beats RWL for comprehension at A2+, Part 2.4). Read-along alone is *not* a justification — the podcast already has it.
5. **Phasing.** **Phase 1 reuses existing content only — no new corpus, no corpus schema:** the story-podcast **transcripts** (`podcasts.transcript_segments`) **and** the **lesson dialogue/narrative text** (201 aligned lines across 16 lessons, already ID/NL/EN). Phase 2 = first-class **read-only longer texts** (storage decision) + new authored content. Roadmap #4-PLUS (podcast-transcript harvest) and #5 (reading harvest) unify into one harvest loop. **Open Phase-1 schema question for the grill:** the vocab→FSRS harvest may be *fully schema-free* if "study this word" just activates the word's **existing** capabilities (reuse `set_lesson_activation`-style RPC) — but becomes schema-touching the moment we track per-learner **reading encounters** (the ~8–12 encounter band) or per-learner **read-state/position**. Decide deliberately; don't add per-learner reading-state until a value-add needs it.
6. **Review requirement.** Because Phase 2 introduces read-only-text storage (generalize `podcasts` → `texts`, or new `reading_texts`, or lesson rows — an open question for the design), the spec **touches the data model** and therefore needs **both `architect` and `data-architect`** sign-off (CLAUDE.md data-model rule), not architect alone.

## Part 4c — Grill decisions (2026-06-28, running log)

- **Q1 — vocab→FSRS harvest is DEFERRED to Phase 2. Phase 1 is schema-free, architect-only.** Finding that forced it: neither existing activation path can schedule a single tapped word without new per-learner state — `learner_lesson_activation` activates a whole lesson (~40 words); `collection_items` membership is **global** (no `user_id`, `migration.sql:3601`), so a learner's personally-tapped words can't be a shared collection. Fine-grained harvest therefore needs new per-learner membership → bundle it with Phase 2's read-only-text storage in one data-architect pass. Phase 1 ships the complete reading surface (silent reading + tap-to-gloss); glossing alone ~doubles incidental uptake, so the goal is reached without harvest. Rejected: A2 coarse harvest (tap → activate whole home lesson) as pedagogically wrong (over-activation); A3 build harvest schema now as premature mechanism for an unproven surface.

- **Q2 — Phase 1 corpus = the existing story-podcast transcripts ONLY (pure reuse, schema-free).** Lesson dialogues are **dropped** from the reader (low marginal value: short, NL-only, no timings, and already visible in the lesson reader). New high-value longer reading content is **Phase 2** (the read-only-stories track + `texts`/`reading_texts` storage + harvest). Phase 1 reads `podcasts.transcript_segments` through a read-time adapter → `ReadableText` view-model. Accepted risk: 8 stories is a thin launch corpus, but it grows with every story-podcast publish and Phase 2 adds dedicated content; enough to validate "does the Read mode get used."

- **Q3 — Read mode is text-primary with an OPTIONAL read-along toggle (evidence-led, not silent-only).** Default OFF at A2+ (silent self-paced beats RWL for comprehension once decoding is fluent), default ON for A1/beginner texts (audio scaffolds decoding + paces reading rate). Highlight = **phrase/sentence-level** (word-level over-binds the eyes — Hui & Godfroid 2026), which also reconciles ADR 0022 (word-level synced follow-along stays the *Listen* face's goal; Read's read-along is phrase-level, so the surfaces don't converge). Frictionless pause + **tap-to-define auto-pauses audio** (answers the #1 RWL quit reason). Speed control. On-demand per-word audio (tap to hear) regardless of toggle. (An earlier "pure silent, no player" rec was corrected — it optimized mode-boundary cleanliness over the RWL evidence; see `feedback_pedagogy_follow_research`.)

- **Q4 — gloss source + coverage are computed against a PROPER known-set, and the existing stories ARE viable (corrected).** A first measurement (exact-match vs staging `learning-items.ts`) wrongly showed A1 stories at ~64–75% coverage and nearly triggered a corpus reversal to lesson dialogues. **That was a stale-file artifact:** `learning-items.ts` omits ~98 grammar-table words (days, question words, pronouns) that were already harvested into the live DB (commit `201c4858`, roadmap §A Track A, audit `docs/audits/2026-06-14-grammar-table-vocab-inventory.md`) plus all function words, and exact match ignores Indonesian morphology. Re-measured against items + lesson vocab sections + function words + morphology-fold: **A1 90.6%, A2 93.3%, B1 88.3%, B2 77.7%** (excl. proper nouns) — the stories sit in the assisted-reading band; gloss the residual 5–10%. **Hard requirement:** the reader's per-learner coverage gate AND the gloss lookup must read **live `learning_items` + a function-word list + morphology root-folding**, NEVER staging `learning-items.ts` (the trap that misled this grill). Proper nouns are skipped (name-detection). **Gloss source (layered, schema-free):** `learning_items.translation_nl/_en` (NL-first) → morphology root gloss → bundled ID→NL/EN dictionary asset for the residual; `item_meanings` is retired (use `translation_nl/_en`). The genuinely-new reader vocab becoming *teachable* (harvested into `learning_items`) is the Phase-2 harvest loop, not Phase 1.
- **Corpus (reaffirmed):** with the stories vindicated, Phase 1 returns to the user's preference — **existing podcast story transcripts**, coverage-ordered (A1→B2), B2 naturally gated out for low-vocab learners. Lesson dialogues stay out unless re-added later.

- **Q5 — gloss interaction = tap-to-reveal (single given gloss), NL-first, in Phase-1 reading.** The effortful "choose-then-verify" (multiple-choice) format is NOT in the reader; it lives in the Phase-2 SRS as the app's **existing `RecognitionMCQ` card** (`recognition_mcq` capability + `recognition_mcq_distractors`), which FSRS already schedules. Rationale (Four Strands): reading = meaning-focused-input strand → low-friction look-up preserves comprehension flow; SRS = deliberate strand → effortful spaced retrieval, where desirable-difficulty research says it belongs and where it already exists. Hard constraint reinforcing it: choose-then-verify needs distractors, which exist only for curated vocab items — words glossed via morphology/dictionary have none, so it could never run uniformly in the reader. **Phase-2 harvest model (confirmed):** looking up a word does NOT auto-add a card; looked-up words are **suggested, learner confirms** (auto-suggest not auto-add, to avoid deck bloat). Already-harvested words → activate existing capabilities; brand-new vocab → must be harvested into `learning_items` first (the heavier half of Phase 2 = schema + content).

- **Q6 — residual gloss fallback = the existing sentence translation; NO bundled dictionary in Phase 1.** Word-gloss from `learning_items.translation_nl/_en` (NL-first) + morphology-root covers ~90%. For the ~10% residual (no item, no root match), tap surfaces the **sentence-level NL translation already in `transcript_segments`** (meaning-in-context, free, schema-free). A bundled ID→NL/EN dictionary is deferred to Phase 1.5/2 (pairs with harvest — a residual word worth glossing is worth harvesting). The "weak full-sentence-gloss" caveat doesn't bite: it's only the residual fallback, not the primary mechanism.

- **Q7 — text leveling = order-by-coverage + readability badge, no hard hiding (Phase 1).** Compute each learner's per-text coverage, sort most-comprehensible-first, show a per-text readability badge (% known / 3-level label); optional soft "~X% new words" note on the hardest. No hard-gating for an 8-text corpus (against the gradient evidence; would empty a beginner's list) — revisit when the corpus is large (Phase 2). **Known set = `learner_capability_state` (introduced/mastering) + function-word list + morphology-fold**, read-side from live state (never staging — [[project_staging_learning_items_drifts_from_db]]). This is research design-principle #1 in minimal correct form.

- **Q8 — module placement: reuse `podcastService` (data) → new `lib/reading/` (domain) → new `components/reading/` + `/lezen` route (UI), under the Input tab.** No parallel `readingService` (foundation plan keeps `podcastService` a service). `lib/reading/` owns the `ReadableText` view-model, per-learner coverage, gloss resolution, ordering/badge. **Deviation flagged:** foundation line 80 has `lib/reading/` importing `session-builder` — that's the *harvest* edge, **Phase 2**; Phase-1 `lib/reading/` imports gloss/coverage lookups only.

### Phase-2 feature — Morphological glossing (added 2026-06-28)

**Goal:** tapping an affixed word shows not just the base word's *meaning* but its *morphology* — the affix, what it does, and the word family — making the reader an in-context surface for the morphology moat (roadmap §D).

- **Phase-1 reality:** the gloss cascade strips affixes with a **crude** runtime stripper (`src/lib/reading/affixStrip.ts`) only to recover a *root that is a `learning_item`* and show its **meaning** (tap `membaca` → "lezen" via `baca`). It does NOT surface the affix, its function, or the family.
- **Phase-2 target:** tap `membaca` → popover shows **`meN-` + `baca` (read) → active verb**, the word family (`baca / membaca / dibaca / pembaca / bacaan`), and a link into the **Affix Trainer** for `meN-`.
- **The work (a real slice, not a tweak):** replace the crude stripper with a **runtime path into the real morphology engine** (`src/lib/morphology/` — affix catalog, `buildAffixDetail`, word-family builder — currently build-time/catalog-oriented; the same runtime gap noted for the Phase-1 gloss), then surface affix→function + family + trainer link in the popover.
- **Fit:** pairs with the harvest (a word understood morphologically is a better SRS candidate); the differentiator the roadmap calls the moat. Distinct slice; can land independently of the harvest.
