## Candidate Story Assessments

### 1. Kura-Kura dan Monyet Yang Rakus
**URL:** https://id.wikibooks.org/wiki/Dongeng/Kura-Kura_dan_Monyet_Yang_Rakus  
**Approximate word count:** ~320 words  
**Theme:** Greed and friendship — a panda and tiger plant banana trees; the greedy tiger eats all the fruit and is punished when the tree falls on him.

**Note:** The page title says "Kura-Kura dan Monyet Yang Rakus" (The Turtle and the Greedy Monkey) but the actual story text features a tiger (Mocan) and panda (Manda) — the characters do not match the title. This suggests a content error or mislabeled page. The story itself is internally consistent.

**A1-A2 suitability:**
- SHORT — ideal podcast length (~3–4 min audio)
- Simple sentence structure, lots of dialogue
- Clear moral lesson at the end (explicitly stated)
- Common vocabulary: trees, fruit, friendship, greed
- Mild mismatch between title and story content is a minor issue

**Rating: 4/5** — Short, simple, dialogue-rich. Title-content mismatch is cosmetically awkward but the story text itself is suitable.

### 2. Balada Ulat Kecil dan Kumbang Madu
**URL:** https://id.wikibooks.org/wiki/Dongeng/Balada_Ulat_Kecil_dan_Kumbang_Madu  
**Approximate word count:** ~600 words  
**Theme:** Perseverance and self-belief — a caterpillar is mocked by a bee for being slow and wingless, but eventually transforms into a beautiful butterfly.

**A1-A2 suitability:**
- MEDIUM length — manageable for podcast (~6–7 min audio)
- Clear, flowing narrative with poetic descriptive language
- Dialogue is natural and natural-paced
- Universal, uplifting theme (metamorphosis as metaphor)
- Vocabulary covers nature: garden, flowers, wings, caterpillar, butterfly, cocoon
- Slightly elevated vocabulary in the descriptive passages ("semedi panjang", "metamorfosa") but these are teachable moments
- Well-structured prose with consistent formatting

**Rating: 5/5** — Best balance of length, vocabulary richness, narrative quality, and beginner-friendliness. The theme is universally relatable.

---

### 3. Si Parkit Raja Parakeet
**URL:** https://id.wikibooks.org/wiki/Dongeng/Si_Parkit_Raja_Parakeet  
**Approximate word count:** ~900 words  
**Theme:** Cleverness and freedom — a parakeet king outwits a hunter and later a king to regain his freedom.

**A1-A2 suitability:**
- LONG — likely 9–10 min audio; may need editing to shorten
- Richly detailed narrative with multiple plot phases
- Involves royalty vocabulary (raja, istana, pengawal, panglima)
- Clever trickster protagonist — engaging story arc
- Vocabulary is slightly more complex (kerajaan context)
- Multiple instances of direct speech make it good for dialogue practice
- Longer story but could be split into two podcast episodes (capture + palace)
- Strong moral: freedom and cleverness prevail over power

**Rating: 4/5** — Excellent story content; length is the main challenge for A1-A2. Could be edited or split.

### 4. Putri Tujuh
**URL:** https://id.wikibooks.org/wiki/Dongeng/Putri_Tujuh  
**Approximate word count:** ~850 words  
**Theme:** Origin story of Dumai city — seven princesses hidden during a war die of starvation; tragic local legend.

**A1-A2 suitability:**
- LONG — ~9 min audio
- Complex vocabulary: war, kingdom, customs, engagement traditions (tepak sirih, combol, adat)
- Tragic ending (all seven princesses die, queen dies of grief)
- Heavy cultural/historical context requires significant background knowledge
- Includes a traditional song/lyric (good for listening)
- NOT recommended for early beginner learners — emotional weight and vocabulary density are too high for A1-A2
- Better suited as A2-B1 material

**Rating: 2/5** — Valuable cultural content but too long, too complex, and too tragic for beginner podcast learners.

---

### 5. Tujuh Anak Lelaki
**URL:** https://id.wikibooks.org/wiki/Dongeng/Tujuh_Anak_Lelaki  
**Approximate word count:** ~1,100 words  
**Theme:** Seven sons abandoned by poor parents during drought find a giantess, escape, build wealth, and reunite with their parents.

**A1-A2 suitability:**
- VERY LONG — ~11–12 min audio; too long for a single episode
- Rich adventure narrative with multiple settings (forest, giant's house, sea voyage, new kingdom)
- Lots of dialogue — good for listening comprehension
- Family vocabulary (anak, suami, istri, orang tua, saudara) is foundational
- Plot complexity and length make it challenging for A1 learners
- Could be adapted or split into 2 parts
- The abandonment-and-reunion theme is emotionally resonant and culturally grounded (Acehnese origin)

**Rating: 3/5** — Good story, rich vocabulary, but too long for a single beginner episode. Could work as a two-part podcast.

### 6. Si Kepar
**URL:** https://id.wikibooks.org/wiki/Dongeng/Si_Kepar  
**Approximate word count:** ~700 words  
**Theme:** A boy named Si Kepar reunites his separated parents through clever scheming, so he will no longer be mocked as a "jazah" (fatherless child).

**A1-A2 suitability:**
- MEDIUM length — ~7–8 min audio
- Character-driven with clear motivation and resolution
- Dialogue-heavy: conversations between Kepar, mother, and father
- Deals with family, divorce, and social stigma — emotionally nuanced but accessible
- Vocabulary: family relationships, farming/mountain life, scheming
- One unusual word: "jazah" (fatherless, a regional term from Aceh Tenggara) — good teaching opportunity
- Well-structured with a satisfying ending

**Rating: 4/5** — Good length, relatable family theme, dialogue-rich. Slightly complex emotional setup but manageable for A2.

---

### 7. Si Monyet Rakus dan Si Katak Dungu
**URL:** https://id.wikibooks.org/wiki/Si_monyet_rakus_dan_si_katak_dungu  
**Approximate word count:** ~1,500 words  
**Theme:** A greedy monkey and a naive frog go to retrieve banana trees from a flooded river; the monkey cheats the frog but they reconcile.

**A1-A2 suitability:**
- VERY LONG — ~15 min audio; far too long for a single episode
- Has a Wikibooks cleanup notice ("perlu dirapikan") — prose is unpolished, inconsistent formatting
- Written in casual, colloquial style with regional dialect elements (from Sumatra/Kalimantan)
- Story-within-a-story framing with a traditional song intro that mixes languages
- Contains a "magic chant" subplot that is creative but adds complexity
- Lots of natural-sounding dialogue — good for advanced listening practice
- NOT suited for A1-A2 in this form — too long, too informal, unedited

**Rating: 2/5** — Charming folk story with authentic oral tradition feel, but needs significant editing before use. Better as source material for a rewritten/adapted version.

---

### 8. Asal Usul Kota Surabaya
**URL:** https://id.wikibooks.org/wiki/Dongeng/Asal_usul_Kota_Surabaya  
**Approximate word count:** ~750 words  
**Theme:** Origin of Surabaya's name — battles between a giant shark (Sura) and a crocodile (Baya) that give the city its name; includes brief historical notes about the Majapahit era.

**A1-A2 suitability:**
- MEDIUM length — ~8 min audio
- Written as a school student's oral presentation (includes "Assalamu'alaikum", personal introduction, "Babay semuanya") — very informal framing
- The narrative core (shark vs. crocodile battle) is vivid and exciting
- Vocabulary: animals, battle, territory, river, sea
- Historical tangent about the Tar-tar army and Raden Wijaya adds complexity unsuited to A1-A2
- School-presentation framing is not podcast-ready and would need to be edited out
- The core legend is one of Indonesia's most well-known stories — high cultural value

**Rating: 3/5** — Core story is culturally important and engaging, but the page needs editing (remove student presentation framing, trim historical digression).
